# Elderly and visa-sensitive guidance

## When to be conservative

Be extra conservative when the traveler is:

- elderly
- traveling alone for the first time in years
- unfamiliar with international transfers
- carrying lots of baggage
- visa-sensitive, especially when transit rights are unclear

## Preferred itinerary characteristics

Strongly prefer:

- one ticket end-to-end
- one stop maximum
- no airport change
- no overnight transfer
- no self-transfer
- layover roughly 2-4 hours
- major hub with straightforward signage

## Extra caution triggers

Treat these as caution flags, not automatic impossibilities:

- transit through a country where entry/transit rules vary by passport, visa, airline, terminal, or overnight status
- separate tickets that might force baggage reclaim and landside check-in
- listings that do not clearly state whether bags are checked through
- unusually cheap itineraries with hidden complexity

## Recommendation language

When rules are not verified, say:

- "This route may be workable, but transit rules should be confirmed before booking."
- "I would not choose this for older travelers unless the airline confirms protected transfer and baggage through-check."

## China-passport heuristic

When the traveler has a Chinese passport and lacks third-country visas/permits, prioritize routes with the least ambiguity. Do not make definitive legal claims unless confirmed from the carrier or official policy. If you cannot verify, label the route as needing transit confirmation.
